Know Your Controls

Before you start — understand what each hand and foot does

📸
images/how-to-ride/controls-overview.jpg

Ảnh xe từ bên cạnh, có chú thích 4 điểm điều khiển: tay trái, tay phải, chân trái, chân phải · 1200×600px

Overview: the 4 controls you need to know

🤚 Left Hand — DO NOTHING

There is no lever on the left side. It's not a brake and not a clutch. Keep it relaxed on the grip.

✋ Right Hand — Throttle + Brake

Twist back = gas. Squeeze lever = front brake. Your main hand for going and slowing.

🦶 Left Foot — Gear Shifter

Toes forward = shift UP (N→1→2→3→4)
Heel back = shift DOWN (4→3→2→1→N)

🦵 Right Foot — Rear Brake

Press down to slow the bike. This is your primary brake — use it more than the front.

The Golden Rule

Master this 3-step rhythm and shifting will feel natural

⚠️

If you don't release the gas while shifting…

The bike will jerk forward violently. Always release the throttle completely before clicking the gear lever.

The 3-Step Rhythm

1

Release

Completely let go of the throttle (right hand). Zero gas.

→
2

Shift

Click the gear lever with your left foot. One click at a time.

→
3

Throttle

Gently twist the gas again to continue riding.

Which Gear to Use?

Choosing the right gear keeps the engine happy and you safe

🎛️
images/how-to-ride/gear-indicator.jpg

Ảnh đồng hồ xe hiển thị số gear N/1/2/3/4, khoanh tròn đỏ vào vị trí chỉ số · 800×500px

The gear indicator on your dashboard — always know which gear you're in

Gear Guide

GearWhen to useSituation
N (Neutral)Stopped onlyWaiting at lights, parking
1stStarting + steep uphillMoving from stop, sharp mountain climbs
2ndSlow roads, tight curvesVillages, hairpin bends, descents
3rdNormal roadsProvincial roads, moderate speed
4thOpen flat roadsHighway, cruising at full speed
💡

Pro tip: Shift DOWN before climbing

Drop to 2nd or 1st gear before you reach a steep hill — don't wait until the engine is struggling. Keeping momentum is much easier than recovering it.

Braking & Downhills

The most important safety skill on mountain roads

🦵
rear-brake-foot.jpg

Cận cảnh chân đạp phanh sau, nền đường núi · 800×600px

Always prioritize the rear brake (right foot)
⛰️
downhill-gear.jpg

Xe xuống đèo, chú thích "Stay in Gear 2–3" · 800×600px

On steep descents — stay in Gear 2 or 3, never Neutral

Braking Rules

  • Prioritize rear brake (right foot) — safer, prevents front-wheel lockup.
  • Never suddenly grab the front brake (right hand lever) — especially on gravel or wet roads. It can lock the front wheel and cause a fall.
  • Use both brakes gently together for smooth, controlled stops.

Engine Braking — For Steep Descents

  • Never use Neutral going downhill. Always stay in Gear 2 or 3.
  • Let the engine naturally slow the bike — this is "engine braking."
  • It prevents your brakes from overheating on long descents (brake fade).
  • Use light, gentle taps on the rear brake to supplement when needed.
🔴

Never coast downhill in Neutral!

With no engine resistance, you pick up speed fast and have only your brakes. On long mountain passes this can cause brake overheating and brake failure. Stay in gear.

⚠️ Practice Before You Go!

The mountain roads of Cao Bang are beautiful — but unforgiving to the unprepared

🅿️
images/how-to-ride/practice-parking.jpg

Ai đó đang tập đi xe trong bãi đỗ, không khí thoải mái và vui · 1200×600px

Take time to practice in our parking area — Sen is always happy to help you get started

🔁 Drill the Golden Rule

Practice Release → Shift → Throttle slowly in the parking area until it feels automatic. 10 minutes here saves hours of struggle on the road.

🦵 Practice Foot Braking

Get used to reaching for the rear brake (right foot) instinctively. In an emergency, your foot should move before you think.

⚖️ Balance at Low Speed

Practice slow turns and figure-eights. Mountain roads have tight hairpin bends where low-speed balance is essential.

🏔️ Get Comfortable, Then Go

Only head out when you feel genuinely confident — not just "good enough." Sen can ride with you for a short test if needed.
